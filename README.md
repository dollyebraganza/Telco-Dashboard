# Telco-Dashboard 
Project Title / Headline
Telecom Customer Churn & Revenue Retention Analytics
Predicting Subscriber Attrition and Safeguarding Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)

2. Short Description & Purpose
The primary goal of this project was to identify the "hidden" drivers of customer churn within a subscription-based business model. By analyzing customer demographics and service usage, I aimed to pinpoint high-risk segments—specifically those on short-term contracts—to provide the leadership team with a data-backed strategy to improve long-term retention and protect company revenue.

3. Tech Stack
Visualization & BI: Power BI Desktop

Data Transformation: Power Query (M Language)

Analytical Logic: Advanced DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)

Data Processing: SQL (for initial data extraction and cleaning)

Design: Professional Dark-Themed UI/UX for Executive Dashboards

4. Data Source
Kaggle: Utilized the Telco Customer Churn dataset, a standardized industry benchmark containing 7,000+ subscriber records, used to simulate real-world telecommunications business scenarios.

Key Dimensions: Customer Demographics (Gender, Senior Citizen status), Account Information (Tenure, Contract Type, Payment Method), and Service Subscriptions (Internet Type, Online Security, Streaming TV).

5. Features & Highlights
Executive KPI Layer: Designed high-visibility cards for Total Customers, Total Revenue, and Churn Rate (%) to provide immediate "at-a-glance" health metrics for the business.

Dimensional Slicing: Implemented interactive side-bar slicers (Gender, Geography, Payment Method) allowing users to "drill down" into specific customer segments in real-time.

Contract-Service Correlation: Utilized Area Charts and Donut Charts to visualize the relationship between contract length and churn, revealing that Month-to-Month users were 3x more likely to leave than long-term subscribers.

Service Impact Analysis: Isolated specific service types (like Fiber Optic vs. DSL) to determine which technical service tiers experienced the highest attrition rates.

Revenue Protection Strategy: Provided a direct link between churn and revenue loss, identifying a specific "Contract Migration" opportunity to move high-risk users to more stable annual plans. 

Business Problem: High customer turnover was draining Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR). The company didn't know if the "leak" was caused by service issues, pricing, or contract types.

Goal of Dashboard: Create a "Retention Command Center" to identify high-risk subscriber personas and provide data-backed strategies to stop churn before it happens.

Key Visuals: * KPI Cards: Total Revenue, Churn Rate, and Total Customers.

Donut/Bar Charts: Comparing Churn across Contract Types and Internet Services.

Area Chart: Tracking the volume of churn over time.

Impact & Insight: Discovered that Month-to-Month subscribers on Fiber Optic plans were the highest risk. Recommended a "Contract Migration" strategy to move these users to annual plans, stabilizing long-term revenue.
